## Structural Issues Summary

1. **MK broad over-dominance (root cause: no SK isolation)** — With only 1 SK campaign, the MK broad campaign is forced to handle all keyword traffic. This means a $0.97 CPC broad match campaign is bidding on exact-match-quality keywords at a 48.8% ACoS instead of a dedicated $0.80 CPC exact match campaign at 25-30% ACoS.

2. **TOS over-aggression across all campaigns** — SK at 199%, MK exact at 158%, MK broad at 114%. With hero keywords already #1 organically, there is zero rank defense justification for these TOS levels. The TOS spending is buying expensive top-of-search clicks that convert worse than rest-of-search.

3. **Budget misallocation** — The best-performing campaign (PT, 37.2% ACoS) is budget-starved at 99.66% utilization with only $7/day. Meanwhile, the MK broad gets $20/day at 48.8% ACoS and the SK gets $35/day at 42.8% ACoS.

4. **Zero negative protection on active campaigns** — The PT campaign ($209.81/30d) has zero negatives, allowing irrelevant terms like "punch needle kit kids" to consume budget.

5. **Dead discovery architecture** — 3 "low bid" campaigns (Auto TOS, MK research, PT low bid) were set up for term discovery but are generating zero actionable data ($0.38 combined spend/30d). The 900% TOS on the Auto TOS campaign is a risk if traffic ever picks up.

6. **Negative profitability** — Seller Board shows this ASIN runs at negative margins. Every point of ACoS improvement is existential, not just optimization.
